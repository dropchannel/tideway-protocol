# Winch Protocol — v0.1

## Overview

The initial Winch protocol defines how two parties exchange encrypted blobs through a
shared relay. One party originates a payload; the other collects it, processes it, and
deposits a response. The relay is stateless beyond the blobs it currently holds and has
no knowledge of message content.

This is the foundational version: asymmetric roles, two fixed slots, delete-on-read
lifecycle, and AES-256-GCM encryption. Later versions generalize the role model and slot
naming.

---

## Roles

**Sender** — originates a payload, deposits it into the relay, then polls for a response.

**Receiver** — polls the relay for incoming payloads, processes them, and deposits
responses back.

The relay is role-agnostic. It stores and serves opaque blobs keyed by channel and slot.
It has no knowledge of which party is the Sender and which is the Receiver.

---

## Channel Model

A **channel ID** is the tenancy primitive. Each channel is an independent namespace
within the relay. Two parties sharing a channel ID and a shared secret constitute a
private communication channel.

In v0.1, a channel has exactly two slots with fixed roles:

- **request slot** — written by Sender, read by Receiver
- **response slot** — written by Receiver, read by Sender

---

## Communication Pattern

**Fire-and-forget with blind polling.** The Sender deposits a payload and receives an
immediate acknowledgement that the deposit was accepted. The Sender then polls separately
for a response. The Receiver polls independently on a configurable interval, receiving
the payload or a no-content signal if nothing is pending.

Neither party maintains an open connection to the relay during the wait period.

---

## Blob Lifecycle

- A blob deposited into a slot persists until explicitly deleted.
- **Slot presence = pending. Slot absence = handled.** No separate queue or delivery log
  is maintained.
- The Receiver's deposit of a response triggers deletion of the request slot. Ordering:
  save response blob first, then delete request blob. If the save fails, the request blob
  remains intact and the Receiver may retry.
- The Sender's retrieval of a response triggers deletion of the response slot.
- The relay is stateless beyond the blobs it currently holds.

---

## Security Properties

- The relay never holds the shared secret and cannot read message content.
- A tampered ciphertext is detectable via AES-GCM authentication tag failure.
- Transport-layer security (e.g. HTTPS) provides an additional layer but is not relied
  upon for confidentiality — termination at the relay does not expose plaintext.

*Encryption algorithm, wire format, and key distribution are defined in the
system-level specification: [`spec/encryption.md`](https://github.com/dropchannel/spec/blob/main/encryption.md).*

---

## Out of Scope (Post-v0.1)

- Generic slot names (addressed in v0.2)
- Symmetric peer roles (addressed in v0.2)
- Multiple channels per deployment
- Authentication or access control on the relay
- Payload size limits
- Retry logic on transport failures
- More than two parties per channel
